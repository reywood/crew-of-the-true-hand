# Tales of the True Hand — 2025-12-17
## Black Banners over Nightstone

---

## [COLD OPEN — 25s]

[MUSIC: low ember bed; wind moving through pine]

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Listen, friend. There is a village near the Ardeep Forest that four heroes bled to save — twice, by my count. And when they came back down out of the trees, provisions on their backs, they lifted their eyes to walls they knew well. And over those walls flew black banners. Black as a raven's wing. Black as a crypt door left ajar.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* The world, friend, does not wait for heroes to come back from the wood.

[STING: sharp low chord, held — 3s]

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — a deer with antlers of gold, an oath sworn to the Prince of the Nine Hells, and a sword in a dead lord's hands that did not wish to be lifted.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

## ACT ONE — The Deer with the Golden Antlers

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture the morning. Outside the goblin cave, two dozen rescued villagers waited at the cave mouth, hungry as only the newly freed can be. So the four went foraging in the wood to fill those mouths. And there, between one tree and the next, stood a deer. A great one — taller at the shoulder than any deer has a right to be. And its antlers, friend, were of gold.

VANDAL: *(measured)* It locked eyes with Eno and stood ready to bolt. Now, Eno was raised in the wild, a cleric of Eldath, goddess of still waters — and if any of the four was going to speak first, it was he. He whispered a Speak with Animals, and said, gently as a man greets a shrine, hello.

VANDAL: *(quoted, quiet)* "Why have you come to this place?"

VANDAL: *(telling)* That is what the deer asked him. And Eno told it the truth — hungry people, an evil in the cave behind them, food wanted for the saving of a village. The deer said nothing more. It turned, walked, paused, and looked back — the way a guide does. And it led them to an orchard of great old trees hung with apples the size of two fists together, food enough for every waiting mouth. Then it ran, and was gone.

VANDAL: *(wondering)* Only a deer, friend. Save that it was not. Mark it. In the great tales, the quiet omens are the ones you remember.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT TWO — The High Steward

VANDAL: *(shifting)* And then the walls, and the black banners, and at the gate a face they knew — Merrick, the guard, delivering the news plain as a man delivers bad weather.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* Lady Nandor was dead — crushed in the cloud giant raid, the very raid these four had answered. And into the empty chair had stepped a man named Corvin. Royal blood, said Merrick. All buttoned up. All legal. He had named himself High Steward of Nightstone, and no one in the village had the standing to say otherwise. The four could enter — once they surrendered their weapons at the gate. And the goblin, of course, could not come in at all. Poor Snigbat, newest scout of the crew, left kicking stones outside the wall.

VANDAL: *(dry)* Daphne — the lady-in-waiting they had themselves pulled out of that goblin cave — confirmed it with a mouth drawn tight as a purse-string. By the laws of Waterdeep, she said, Corvin was within his rights. Cold comfort, the way she said it.

VANDAL: *(unfolding)* The four were granted an audience, and here is the strange part, friend. Corvin cared little for the grievances of one small village. His eye was on the whole burning map of the North. Frost giants pressing down on Bryn Shandar in the far icy north. Fire giants at Triboar and the Golden Fields, digging great holes in the earth — as if hunting for something buried and old. The giant troubles were not one village's sorrow. They belonged to everyone.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT THREE — Oaths in the Square

VANDAL: *(gathering)* Corvin called for oaths. Every soul who meant to remain in Nightstone would swear him loyalty in the square, and Toz and Fiz went down to witness — halfling and gnome, watching their neighbours line up under black banners to give away pieces of themselves.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Most swore plainly. A crop, a hearth, a roof, a lord. But one woman drew every eye. Aganor, a tiefling, swore her oath to no earthly seat at all — but to Asmodeus. The archdevil himself, friend. Lord of the Nine Hells, who once reached for all the Realms and was only barely thwarted. She said it in the open square, and the square went very quiet.

VANDAL: *(dropping)* And a family of three — the Delfryndels — would not swear at all. They walked out past the gate and chose the wild over words they would not say. In times like these, an unbought conscience is a rare coin.

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Now, Aganor had a quarrel of her own. Corvin's men had seized her cottage — a locked little house with runes of Infernal burned row upon row into the door — and inside it, on a small altar by the window, sat her spellcasting focus. A statuette of a devilish figure, the very channel through which her magic flows. Help me take it back, she told the two, and I will owe you.

VANDAL: *(amused, telling)* So Toz and Fiz called first on Xolkin — the Zhentarim man with the winged snake, who had no love left for the new lord, and less for the world since the beautiful Kella slipped away in the night and took his heart with her. And then they strolled to the seized cottage, nonchalant as two fellows admiring the weather. A guard stood at the door. The window stood open. And Fiz — small Fiz of Halruaa — cast a Mage Hand shaped like a little mechanical bird, and floated that devil-idol clean out the window, past the guard's shoulder, into his waiting satchel.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* The focus went back into Aganor's hands. A debt owed now, friend. Uncollected. But owed.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT FOUR — The Crypt Beneath the Wall

VANDAL: *(dropping into serious)* And while the village swore above, Hal and Eno went underneath it. For there was a secret way into Nightstone — a well outside the walls, and at the bottom of the well, a passage no guard of Corvin's had ever stood watch over.

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Down the ladder they went, torch in hand, the webs growing thicker with every rung — until a giant spider reared up out of the dark, its head the size of a man's. The paladin and the cleric put it down between them and pressed on, into a small stone mausoleum. One room. One sarcophagus, carved with the likeness of a sword, holding some long-dead lord of Nightstone. And the way onward into the crypt proper — sealed by a great slab of stone.

VANDAL: *(measured)* The plan, friend, was a rendezvous. Come nightfall, Toz and Fiz would work that slab from the village side with a crowbar. But while they waited, Hal — Hal Stormguard, sworn to the Oath of Vengeance — set his hands to the sarcophagus lid, and pried it.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* Within lay a body gone mostly to bone. And in its fingers, a sword — gleaming, friend, after untold years in the dark. More than steel. And as Hal looked on the dead lord's face, the dead lord looked back.

VANDAL: *(cold)* A ghost rose out of the corpse, and it was angry.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Eno tried words first — we come to save your town, he said, we mean no plunder. The dead do not bargain. So the cleric raised his holy symbol and called his Channel Divinity down the narrow room — Turn Undead, the still-water light of Eldath — and the ghost recoiled from it, wailing, and fled back into its own bones.

VANDAL: *(taut)* But a ghost turned is not a ghost destroyed. They had minutes, no more. Hal took the sword from the dead man's fingers, and the two of them threw themselves at the slab — while on the far side Toz and Fiz heaved on the crowbar, and Toz, bless him, slipped and broke his nose on the stone for his trouble. Then the slab ground open, and the crew stood reunited, bloodied nose and all, before the dead lord could rise again.

VANDAL: *(grave)* And in Hal's fist, the prize. A fine blade, and a wrong one. There was a chill in the hilt that had nothing to do with the crypt. He is a paladin, friend. He knows the shape of a curse when he holds one.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT FIVE — The Roads Out

VANDAL: *(reflective)* So count the day's ledger with me. A tiefling's debt, half-paid and owing. A sword that will ask a price for every swing. A village under a High Steward whose right no one can deny, and whose rule no one loves. And two long roads pointing out from Nightstone's gate.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* North, to Bryn Shandar, where the frost giants press. Or south and west, to Triboar and the Golden Fields, where the fire giants dig — and where, if you recall old Molak's parting gift, a leader named Derothra Shendrel is said to keep the true name of the genie in the crew's salvaged bottle.

VANDAL: *(softer)* Which road, friend? The one where folk are freezing, or the one where the earth is being opened? And somewhere back in the wood, a golden-antlered deer waits to see which way they turn.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* A cursed sword in the hand of a vengeful man. A debt owed by a woman sworn to Asmodeus. A family walking into the wild rather than kneel. A lord on a stolen chair, and a village that has not decided whether to keep him. That, friend, is what one day in the North looks like now.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. Two roads wait at the gate, and only one dawn to choose.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
