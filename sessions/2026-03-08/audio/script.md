# Tales of the True Hand — 2026-03-08
## The Tree That Named a Giant

---

## [COLD OPEN — 25s]

[MUSIC: low ember bed; a soft wind moving over ripe wheat]

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Listen, friend. South of a farming town in the North there stands a grove of trees that are not entirely trees. A druid passed that way, decades gone, and spoke the old spell — Awaken — over their roots. And then he walked on, and left them thinking. The children of the town climb their branches and carve their names into the bark, and the trees permit it. And on the day I'll tell you of, four travelers came into that grove with a question they had carried since Nightstone. Where is the Oracle? And the eldest tree considered them a long while. And answered.

VANDAL: *(quoted, cold, hushed)* "You will need a giant. His name is Harshnag."

[STING: sharp low chord, held — 3s]

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — from an account I have gathered since, for I was not there — a cloud giant sets our heroes down among the wheatfields of Golden Fields, where a yeti pours the ale, a tree grants an audience, and every road in the North begins to bend toward Waterdeep.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

---

## ACT ONE — Set down among the wheat

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture it. A tower of cloud and stone drifting low over the granary-lands of the North, its shadow sliding across the ripening wheat. Zephyros — the cloud giant in purple robes, hair of cloud, mind gone gently sideways from long converse with the outer planes — set the four heroes down at the edge of the town called Golden Fields. And before his tower rose away into the blue, he gave them a thread to pull should they ever need him again.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* In Silverymoon, he said, seek out one Baros Ravenkind. Baros can fashion a silver eagle — an Animal Messenger, keyed to the giant himself, that will find his tower wherever the winds have carried it. A lifeline on a very long string, friend. They tucked the name away.

VANDAL: *(plain)* The guards at the gate were polite, and bright, and entirely unworried. All was well in hand, they said. No help needed. And the four — who have learned by now what a town sounds like just before it learns otherwise — thanked them kindly, and kept their own counsel, and walked in.

VANDAL: *(softer)* At the heart of the town stood a wooden statue of the goddess Shontea — plump, smiling, ringed round with sheaves of wheat. A farmer's goddess for a farming town, and the town twice the size of Nightstone. Which is to say, friend: a granary. And a granary, in a season when giants have grown hungry, is never as safe as its gate-guards believe.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT TWO — The yeti behind the bar

VANDAL: *(amused, unfolding)* At the inn, the bar was tended by a yeti. I shall say that once more, plainly, and let it sit. The bar was tended by a yeti. Great white shag on him, a rag in one enormous paw, and a name — Miros.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Now most travelers stop in the doorway at a sight like that. But Eno — the half-elf among them, cleric of Eldath, raised in the wild before ever the Greenbottles took him in — Eno walked up to the bar and greeted the creature in his own tongue. In Yeti. And Miros lit up like a hearth in midwinter. A free room appeared. Cups appeared. And with the cups came everything the gate-guards had not said.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* Hill giants had been raiding the region for food. Two weeks past they had come in the dead of night — goblins and bugbears slipping over the walls to carry off grain, while the giants stood beyond bowshot and hurled stones at the ramparts. The captain of the guard, a half-orc named Strog Thunderblade, was reckoned lazy even by the charitable. The real authority sat with the Castellan, one Tarn. And Miros himself — mark this, friend — Miros was sworn to the Emerald Enclave. A yeti in a barkeep's apron, keeping the wild's watch over a town of wheat.

VANDAL: *(sly, amused)* A halfling bard named Orin held the corner that evening — a colleague, though I'll not vouch for his repertoire — and he offered two things worth the hearing. First, a warning: leave the awakened trees outside town alone. And second, an aside: a woman had lately come to Golden Fields, alone, and taken up work as a shepherd. A small thing. But in a town where giants come by night, friend, no small thing stays small for long.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT THREE — Ink on her fingers

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* At a corner table sat a woman alone over her notes. Plain clothes, finely cut. Ink stains on her fingers — a scholar's stains, not a farmer's. Miros leaned across the bar and offered Eno, in Yeti, an opinion of Waterdhavian nobility that I shall not translate by a fireside. But Hal and Fiz went over all the same.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Naxene, by name. Of Waterdeep. Stand-offish at first — but Hal, earnest Hal, paladin of the Oath of Vengeance and once of the Silver Marches militia, has the plain soldiering manner that softens the wary. And soften she did. She had been crossing the North, she told them, tracking giant raids from town to town. And what she had found dismayed her. Not one raid. Not a dozen. A pattern — everywhere — and every kind of giant setting hand to it. The Ordning, friend. The old ranking of giantkind, broken, and all its pieces in motion at once.

VANDAL: *(leaning, telling)* Her own conviction, spoken quietly, was that the good dragons of Faerûn — the silvers, the golds, the bronzes, old wisdoms on old wings — might yet be brought in as allies against what was coming. And to that end she pressed a sealed letter into the crew's hands. To be carried to Waterdeep, and delivered to a wizard she trusted. Chazlauth, his name. Remember it, friend. I promise you it repays the remembering.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT FOUR — The grove grants an audience

VANDAL: *(wondering, drawing close)* And then — heeding Orin's directions rather more than his warning — the four walked south of town, past the abbey, and in among the awakened trees. And the eldest and wisest of them stirred, and looked down, and gave his name. Lifferloss.

VANDAL: *(warm)* He looked longest at Eno. And then and there, the old tree invited the wild-raised cleric to join the Emerald Enclave — the same brotherhood as Miros behind his bar, the same as the elf druid Daniel Windraven, whose vine bracelet Fiz wears to this day. Eno has belonged to the wild since before he had words for it, friend. The Enclave was only asking him to make it official.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* Then the crew asked their question, and Lifferloss answered it fully. The Oracle dwells in the far cold north, at the Spine of the World — and they would not reach it alone. They would need Harshnag. A frost giant, friend — hear that rightly — a frost giant who turned his back on his own kind and swore himself to the defense of the small folk. He rode with Force Gray, the famous adventuring company of Waterdeep: Meloon Wardragon fought beside him, and Laeral Silverhand herself, who rules that city now, worked closely with the company in its day. Force Gray is disbanded, its members scattered. But a statue of Harshnag still stands in Waterdeep. And where a statue stands, someone remembers the man — or the giant.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* Count with me, friend. A cloud giant. A yeti. A noblewoman. And now a tree. Four voices in two days, and every one of them pointing west, to the City of Splendours.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## ACT FIVE — The abbot in dusty robes

VANDAL: *(reflective, unfolding)* Last of all they called upon the abbot — the head of the whole town, robes dusty to the knee from walking his own fields, a follower of Shontea like all his flock, a man who counts honest work as prayer. At his elbow, his assistant fretted: the guard is not equal to the giants, something must be done. And the abbot smiled, and shook his head.

VANDAL: *(quoted, dropping)* "All is well in hand."

VANDAL: *(softer, telling)* The same words as the gate. And Eno — taking a cleric's quiet measure of the lazy captain, the fretful assistant, and the good gentle man who would not turn to look at the horizon — offered the only answer Eldath teaches for such a moment. He offered his back. To the harvest. To the work in front of him. Because you cannot make a town believe the giants are coming, friend. You can only be standing in it when they do.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so a chapter closes in a granary-town twice the size of Nightstone — a yeti behind the bar, a tree in the grove, and a noble's sealed letter riding in a gnome's coat pocket. Ahead of the four: Waterdeep. A wizard named Chazlauth. A statue of a frost giant, and whatever remains of Force Gray. Silverymoon in time, and Baros Ravenkind, and a silver eagle on a long string. And behind them, always, Golden Fields — a lazy captain, a sleeping abbot, walls full of grain. And a shepherdess newly come to town, whose name nobody thought to ask.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The road waits at dawn, and this time it runs west.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
