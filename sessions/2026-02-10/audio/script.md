## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — an account I have gathered from the four who lived it, and from a cloud giant who fought his first dragon beside them — lightning over the wheat-roads, a rider with aims of his own, and a voice beneath a sleeping sea.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

## ACT ONE — The voice beneath the glass

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture it. Zephyros's flying tower riding a favourable wind through the night, and inside it, four heroes taking a well-earned rest. And Toz Greenbottle — halfling captain of the wrecked *True Hand* — dreamed again of the glassy ocean. Only this time, friend, he was not alone in it. Beneath the smooth dark water a shape moved. Vast. Amorphous. Malevolent. And it spoke to him in a voice from the bottom of the world.

VANDAL: *(quoted, cold, hushed)* "Your god no longer watches over you. I can sense his absence. Worship me, and my power will be yours."

VANDAL: *(dropping)* The driftwood under his feet began to sink. Cold water took his ankles. And the voice said — *to drown is to be reborn.*

VANDAL: *(measured)* Toz woke to a giant's hand shaking the tower awake. Zephyros had spent the dark hours in trance, casting Contact Other Plane — reaching across the void to the friends he keeps out there among the stars. And what came back to him was this. That the giant gods themselves — the old gods, the true gods — wished to aid the crew. But not yet. Not until the four had proven themselves at a place called the Eye of the All-Father, which some name the Eye of Annam, where an oracle waits who can answer any question set before her. Seek it out, the voices warned, before facing any lord of giantkind. To do otherwise would court disaster.

VANDAL: *(darker)* And one thing more, said the giant. Great peril. Coming this way. Already on the wind.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT TWO — Down to the ground

VANDAL: *(taut, unfolding)* And there it was, out on the horizon. A storm — but no storm the sky had made. Bluish-mauve, laced through with lightning that walked sideways in the cloud, and no forecast in the giant's charts had breathed a word of it. An hour and a half out. Closing.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Below stretched the rolling farmland of Golden Fields, the walled town small in the distance. And Toz, a captain to his bones, put the matter plainly: a tower a thousand feet in the air is a murder-box when murder comes flying. Set us down. So Zephyros sank his spire to a rocky outcrop beside a little wooded grove — and a farmer's wagon on the road took one look at a castle descending from the heavens, wheeled about, and fled. Fair play to him, friend. So would I.

VANDAL: *(unfolding)* Then Fiz — small Fiz of Halruaa, tinker and artificer — sent a charmed griffin up into the weather to scout. Back it came, bowing its beaked head. *Master. Three stone-like monsters with wings. A small flying dragon-ish thing. And one big blue lizard — with some sort of dragon man on his back.*

VANDAL: *(dropping)* Three gargoyles. A winged kobold. A young blue dragon. And a half-dragon rider in a deep blue cloak. So the crew dug in — the giant's great desk overturned for a rampart, his levitating disc set as a wall, Hal on the upper floor, and Eno, quiet cleric of Eldath, laying Bless and Barkskin over his companions like a blessing before battle. And the doorway held its breath.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT THREE — Little mice in a mouse hole

VANDAL: *(grave)* The dragon landed with a thud that rattled the outcrop, and named his price at once — the blood of the giant. Fiz tried to parley, small voice from a dark doorway, and the beast only laughed at it.

VANDAL: *(quoted, dry)* "Come out and speak with me, whoever keeps asking questions. Like little mice inside of a mouse hole."

VANDAL: *(measured)* There was no bargain to be had. *I just want to kill a giant*, said the dragon. *That is all.* And Zephyros — purple-robed, grim, gripping his staff — told the crew a truth as old as the Realms. Dragons and giants are ancient nemeses, friend. They warred for thousands of years, until the Ordning gave the world its shape. And now the Ordning is shattered — and it is utter chaos. The giant had trained for this all his long life. He had also, he admitted, never once fought a dragon.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Then the rider showed his hand. Two blue ethereal wings unfurled from his back, and he broke away from the beast entirely — flying not at the crew, but at the tower's upper floors, on some errand all his own. Fiz took him out of the sky with a single Fire Bolt. The half-dragon's concentration shattered, his wings guttered out like a snuffed lamp, and down he came, sixty feet, into the wet grass. Toz was on him before he could rise — a dagger to the heart. And as the rider died, the glass vial of blue liquid at his throat turned, in one breath, to ash.

VANDAL: *(taut)* The dragon answered with a line of lightning thick as a ship's mast, straight at Zephyros — who shrugged it from his shoulders like rain. But out on the wing of the fight, the gargoyles caught one of the tower's griffins and tore it to bloody pieces.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT FOUR — A bloody trade in the rain

VANDAL: *(unfolding, quickening)* And then, friend, the long grind of it. Zephyros conjured an air elemental — a living whirlwind, set spinning at the dragon's flank. Eno's Guiding Bolts came one after another, painting the beast in radiant light. Toz laid Ray of Frost across the winged kobold. And Hal — oath-sworn Hal, in full plate — took a running start and leapt nine feet straight up into the storm, Hunter's Mark burning on the beast, and drove his blade into the dragon's eye. Twice he struck. The dragon did not fall.

VANDAL: *(dropping, taut)* What fell was Hal. The breath weapon came again — a river of lightning down the line — and the paladin dropped where he stood, a griffin dead beside him, Toz scorched and reeling. Hal lay dying in the mud while gargoyles raked at Fiz, and the dragon, nine parts dead itself, clawed skyward to flee.

VANDAL: *(urgent)* It did not get far. Fiz burned it with Scorching Ray as it rose. And Zephyros — the arcane researcher who had never fought a dragon — opened his hand and let fly a flurry of Magic Missiles that no scale can turn. The great blue beast came down out of its own storm, dead before it struck the earth — and landed, I am told, a hand's width from where Hal lay bleeding.

VANDAL: *(amused, softer)* The kobold bolted for the clouds with its master's satchel. A griffin ran it down sixty feet up and brought the bag home like a good hound. And the last gargoyle — well. Zephyros cast Mass Suggestion upon it, and said, with a giant's weary courtesy: *please leave my tower.* And it did.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Eno knelt in the rain and spoke a Healing Word, and Hal opened his eyes to the grey sky. Bloodied, friend. Two griffins the poorer. But alive — every one.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT FIVE — The spoils, and the questions

VANDAL: *(warm, telling)* Now — the picking of a dragon is an old trade, and the crew set to it in the rain. From the rider's body Toz claimed a Bloodwell Vial — a sorcerer's focus, fed by a drop of the bearer's own blood, that sharpens the aim of a spell and restores a well-rested sorcerer's power — and a Dagger of Venom besides. The kobold's satchel — immaculate leather on a filthy little creature, mark that — proved a Bag of Holding. And inside: eighty gold pieces. A potion of lightning resistance. Two Scrolls of Tongues. Blue-steel thieves' tools etched with Draconic numerals. A silk pouch of powdered sapphire worth a hundred and fifty gold at any honest table. A ring, plain and unmagical, cut with the sigil of a blue dragon. And a sheaf of notes, written close and careful, in Draconic — which not one soul aboard can read.

VANDAL: *(amused)* From the dragon itself they drew off two liters of blood into a giant-sized jar. A potent stimulant, Zephyros warned them, and viciously addictive — but a fine thing to sell in the right port. Fiz pocketed a single intact tooth for his trouble. And somewhere in the counting of it all, the four felt the road turn beneath them — for they had crossed, that day, into a new season of their skill.

VANDAL: *(dropping, reflective)* But mark the questions, friend. The rider was no servant of that dragon — he flew for the tower with aims of his own, and died with a vial that unmade itself. His notes are unread. His ring is unclaimed. And no one yet knows who sends such riders, or why.

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so a chapter closes, in the rain outside Golden Fields, beside the cooling corpse of a dragon and a giant who has now seen one fall. Ahead of the four — the Eye of Annam, wherever in the wide North it may lie, and an oracle who will answer any question they can carry to her. Ahead of them, nearer still — the walls of Golden Fields, watching. And behind Toz's eyes, each time they close — a glassy sea, and a shape beneath it, patient as the tide, still making its offer. Mark that voice, friend. You will hear it again.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The mud will hold your boot-prints till morning.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
