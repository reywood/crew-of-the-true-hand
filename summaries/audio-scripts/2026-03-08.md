# Tales of the True Hand — 2026-03-08
## A Yeti, a Tree, and a Noble's Letter

---

## [COLD OPEN — 25s]

[MUSIC: low ember bed; a soft wind moving over ripe wheat]

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Listen, friend. There is a grove of trees outside a farming town in the North, and those trees were woken, long ago, by a druid passing through — and forgotten by him after. And on a certain autumn morning, four small heroes stepped between their trunks, and one of the trees leaned down. It looked at the half-elf among them. And in a voice like bark splitting slow, it named a giant. And it named the Spine of the World. And it named the Oracle they had already been sent to find.

VANDAL: *(quoted, cold, hushed)* "You will need Harshnag," said the tree. "And Harshnag walks now in Waterdeep — or in stone above it."

[STING: sharp low chord, held — 3s]

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — from an account I have since gathered, for I was not there — a cloud giant sets our four heroes down among the wheatfields of Golden Fields, and a yeti behind a bar, and an awakened tree in a grove, and a noblewoman with ink on her fingers, all point them the same way. West. To Waterdeep. And to a giant named Harshnag.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

## [ACT ONE — The tower sets them down]

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture it. A cloud tower drifting low over the granary-fields of the North, its curtain hem trailing mist over the ripening wheat. The purple-robed cloud giant Zephyros — hair of cloud, brain by his own admission a little addled by his long consorting with the planes — set the four heroes gently down at the edge of the town called Golden Fields. And before he took his leave, he pressed on them a way to call him back.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* In Silverymoon, he said, they must seek out one Baros Ravenkind. Baros could craft them a silver eagle — an animal messenger, keyed to the giant, that would find him wherever his tower drifted. A lifeline, friend, on a long string. The crew tucked it away, and Zephyros drifted up and away into the high blue.

VANDAL: *(warm)* The guards at the gate of Golden Fields were polite. Bright-plated, and confident. They needed no help, they said. All was well in hand. And the four heroes — being the sort who have learned, by now, to keep their own counsel — nodded, and thanked them, and walked in.

VANDAL: *(softer)* At the centre of the town stood a wooden statue of the goddess Shontea. Plump. Smiling. Ringed round with sheaves of wheat. A farmer's goddess, for a farming town. And the town itself — twice the size of Nightstone. Which is to say — friend — big enough to be worth taking, if you were a giant come down from the hills.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## [ACT TWO — The yeti behind the bar]

VANDAL: *(amused, unfolding)* At the inn, the bar was tended by a yeti. Now — I say that plainly, and I let it sit. The bar was tended by a yeti. Great white shag on him, and a rag in his paw, and a name — Miros. And the four heroes stopped in their tracks.

VANDAL: *(warm)* But Woz — half-elf Woz, cleric of Eldath, raised in the wild before ever the Greenbottles took him in — Woz stepped up to the bar and greeted the yeti in his own tongue. In Yeti. And the great furred creature laughed, and clapped him on the shoulder, and gave the crew a room for the night, free of charge.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* And then, over cups, Miros told them what the guards at the gate had not. Hill giants had been raiding the region. Two weeks past, they had come in the middle of the night — goblins and bugbears creeping the walls to make off with grain, while the giants themselves stood outside and threw stones at the ramparts. The captain of the guard, a half-orc named Strog Thunderblade, was reckoned lazy. The real authority sat with the Castellan — one Tarn. And Miros himself, friend — Miros was of the Emerald Enclave. Mark that. A yeti, in a barkeep's apron, sworn to the wild.

VANDAL: *(sly, amused)* A halfling bard was there too — Orin by name — who warned the crew off a grove of trees outside town. Awakened, he said. Best left alone. And in the same breath he mentioned, almost as an aside, a woman newly arrived in Golden Fields who had taken up work as a shepherd. A small thing. But worth filing away, friend. In a town where giants come by night, no small thing stays small for long.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## [ACT THREE — The noble with ink on her fingers]

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* At a corner table sat a woman alone. Plain clothes, but the cut of them was fine. And on the pads of her fingers — ink stains. Not a farmer's stains, friend. A scholar's. A noble's. Fiz and Hal, being the two of the crew most inclined toward polite approach, went over.

VANDAL: *(warm)* She was stand-offish at first. Naxene, by name. Of Waterdeep. But Hal — earnest Hal, paladin of Vengeance and once of the Silver Marches militia — has the soldiering manner that softens the wary, and softens her she did. She had been travelling the North, she said, tracking giant activity from Nightstone to Bryn Shandar and back. And what she had found dismayed her. It was not one raid. It was not two. It was a pattern, everywhere, and every kind of giant setting hand to it.

VANDAL: *(leaning, telling)* Her own conviction, she said quietly, was that the good dragons of Faerûn might yet be allies in what was coming. Silver. Gold. Bronze. Old wisdoms with wings. And to that end, she pressed into the crew's hands a folded letter, sealed. To be delivered, in Waterdeep, to one Chazlauth. A wizard, friend. A wizard she trusted. Remember that name.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## [ACT FOUR — The awakened tree]

VANDAL: *(wondering, drawing close)* And then — friend — heeding Orin's directions rather than his warning, the crew walked out of Golden Fields and into the grove of the awakened trees. And one tree among them was the eldest and the wisest. Lifferloss, by name.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Lifferloss looked at Woz — the wild-raised cleric among them — and invited him, then and there, to join the Emerald Enclave. Miros the yeti was of it. Lifferloss was of it. And Woz, quiet Woz, has always belonged to it in his bones, if not yet on paper.

VANDAL: *(warm, telling)* And then the crew asked the tree the question they had been carrying since Nightstone. Where, they asked, is the Oracle? And Lifferloss answered. The Oracle, said the tree, dwells at the Spine of the World, in the far cold north. But you will not reach it alone. You will need a giant. His name is Harshnag. He once rode with Force Gray — an adventuring company of Waterdeep, long since disbanded. A statue of him still stands in the city. And other former members of that company may yet be found there. Start there, said the tree. Start in Waterdeep.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* Three sources now, friend. A cloud giant, a yeti, and a tree. All pointing the same way.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## [ACT FIVE — The abbot in dusty robes]

VANDAL: *(reflective, unfolding)* Last, they called on the abbot. Robes dusty from walking his own fields — a follower of Shontea, like the rest of his flock, who counted honest work as prayer. His assistant fretted at his elbow. The guard, said the assistant, was not equal to the giants. Something must be done.

VANDAL: *(measured, dropping)* The abbot smiled, and shook his head. All was well in hand, he said. All was well.

VANDAL: *(softer, telling)* And Woz, taking a cleric's measure of the room — of the sleeping guard captain, and the fretful assistant, and the abbot who had not yet turned to look at the horizon — offered the only thing a cleric of Eldath knows to offer when the storm is coming and no one will hear it. He offered his back. To the harvest. To the work in front of him. Because you cannot make a town believe a giant is coming, friend. You can only be there when it does.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so a chapter closes, in a farming town twice the size of Nightstone, with a yeti behind the bar and a tree in the grove and a noble's letter in a small gnome's pocket. Ahead of the four — Waterdeep. A wizard named Chazlauth. A statue of a giant named Harshnag, and the scattered remnants of Force Gray. Silverymoon, in time, and Baros Ravenkind and his silver eagle. And behind them, always — Golden Fields. A lazy captain. A sleeping abbot. And a shepherdess newly come to town, whose name none of them yet knows.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The road waits for you at dawn, and this time it runs west.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
