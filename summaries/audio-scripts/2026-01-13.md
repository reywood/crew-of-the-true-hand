## [ACT ONE — The fall of Corvin]

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture it. The streets of Nightstone, thick with smoke and shouting. Toz — small Toz Greenbottle, captain of the wrecked *True Hand* — whirls a Dust Devil through Corvin's bandit guard. Hal wades in with his new sword, still learning its weight. And the tiefling Aganor — bound that very day by an oath she had already begun to regret — hurls Burning Hands at the men she had once called comrades.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Corvin, the would-be royal lordling, tries to slip the noose. He goes invisible. But Fiz — small Fiz of Halruaa — paints him in violet Fairy Fire. Toz's Thunderwave lays his lackeys flat. And in a flurry of bandit blades and devil claws — and, at the last, Aganor's own dagger — Corvin the usurper dies face-down in the dirt of the village he had stolen.

VANDAL: *(quieter, dropping)* And that, friend, ought to have been the end of it. But oaths in the Realms are heavier than iron. And Aganor had sworn hers to Asmodeus himself.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## [ACT TWO — The Cambion's bargain]

VANDAL: *(grave, unfolding)* The ground split. Four imps of Hell clawed up into the air. And behind them came a tall figure in silver plate, with a devil's horns and a courtier's smile. He named himself Destiny. A cambion, sent up from the Nine Hells to collect what Aganor had promised.

VANDAL: *(measured)* She had sworn — poor foolish Aganor — never to raise a hand against Corvin. In killing him, she had forfeited her soul. And Destiny, all courtesy, laid out her wages. Several hundred years, he said, as a slug-creature in the pits below. He offered to do it, and I quote, *the fun way*.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* The crew tried to talk. They tried to bargain a delay. When Aganor's nerve broke and she lunged at him — well. There was nothing left but the running of it. Toz threw a Fog Cloud across the cambion's eyes. Greases went down. Spike Growths went up. Fiz tossed off small healings as they backed toward the gate, and the cambion's eye-beams burned Hal to the bone.

VANDAL: *(warm)* But Destiny wanted Aganor, and only Aganor. The fog held. The gate switches flipped. And the crew fled into the dark and waited him out, one hard-earned short rest under cover, listening for the sound of hooves that never came.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* When they crept back at last, the cambion was gone. Aganor was still alive — profusely apologetic, and at last honest about the oath she had broken. Her soul is forfeit still, friend. And a cambion, once summoned, is not so easily unsummoned.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## [ACT THREE — A town repaid]

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Nightstone, by default, was theirs. And Molot and the grateful villagers — with the merchant Corvin had murdered no longer around to object — opened their storerooms wide.

VANDAL: *(bright, telling)* Four draft horses, and a wagon to pull behind them. A hundred and fifty gold, eighty-five silver, and a good round sack of copper. Three gems worth fifty gold apiece. A potion of healing. A scroll of Continual Flame. Mason's tools. A fine bottle of Dragon Breath whiskey — mark that one, friend, for later mischief. A cask of ale. And food enough for a long road.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Xolkin, the Zhentarim man, pulled back his sleeve and showed the winged-snake tattoo on his arm. He muttered darkly of one Kella, who had *stolen* something from him. And he pressed a magical trinket into the crew's hands as thanks — its nature not yet known.

VANDAL: *(quieter, reflective)* Fiz walked to the pit where the Nightstone itself had stood, and cast what small divinations he could into the empty ground. And Hal, examining the blade he had drawn from the crypt, confirmed at last what the sword truly was — enchanted, yes, but cursed also. It leaves its bearer mistrustful. And it turns every elf who meets its bearer just a little bit cold.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## [ACT FOUR — A giant in purple robes]

VANDAL: *(wondering, drawing close)* And then, friend — as the four sat over a map, arguing where next to sail — Triboar for the genie-bottle lead, Bryn Shandar in the icy north, or the great city of Waterdeep — the sky itself parted above them.

VANDAL: *(theatrical, storyteller)* Down came a tower. A flying tower. And in its topmost window stood a cloud giant in robes of deep purple, with hair made — I say this plainly, friend, though you may not believe me — hair made of clouds. He named himself Zephyros. And he named the four before him *the ones who will restore the Ordning*.

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* Now — the Ordning. Mark the word. It is the ranking of giantkind, from cloud to storm to fire to frost to hill and stone, and it has stood since the first days. It is shattered. King Hekaton, lord of the storm giants, has vanished into the sea. His daughters, at Maelstrom in the deep, are trying and failing to hold the world together. And to reach them — friend — one must have a conch shell from a giant lord, and speak into it from below the waves.

VANDAL: *(amused, softer)* Zephyros admitted, gently, that his long consorting with beings of the outer planes had *affected his brain*. "But," he said, "it has been worth it." And he offered them passage in his tower.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so, friend, the prologue ends. Corvin is dust. Aganor lives, but her soul is spoken for. Xolkin's grudge still simmers. Hal carries a cursed blade he cannot yet lay down. And in the sky above Nightstone, a purple-robed giant waits at the door of a flying tower, offering passage to four small heroes who have just been named — by a stranger from the clouds — the ones who will set the giants right.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. Storm King's Thunder begins at dawn.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
