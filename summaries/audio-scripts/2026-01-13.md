## ACT ONE — The Fall of Corvin

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Now this tale, friend, I have from those who lived it — I was not yet part of their story, nor they of mine. But picture it as it was told to me. The streets of Nightstone, thick with smoke and shouting. Corvin — a man of royal blood, mind you, and a claim just legal enough to stink — had taken the village for his own while its dead were still warm. And the Crew of the True Hand had decided, at last, that he was done ruling it.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Toz Greenbottle, captain without a ship, whirled a Dust Devil through Corvin's bandit guard — a snarling column of wind and grit that flung armed men about like laundry. Hal Stormguard waded in with the blade he had pulled from the crypt beneath the village, still learning its weight and not yet knowing its price. And beside them fought Aganor — the tiefling sorceress of Nightstone, sworn that very day to the crew's cause — pouring Burning Hands into men she had lately called neighbours.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Corvin, seeing how the wind blew, did what usurpers do. He vanished. Invisibility, friend — a coward's second skin. But Fiz of Halruaa, smallest of the four, threw out a Fairy Fire, and the lordling lit up violet in the smoke like a festival lantern. Toz's Thunderwave laid his lackeys flat. And then the blades came in — bandit steel, devil claws, and last of all Aganor's own dagger, driven home.

VANDAL: *(quieter, dropping)* Corvin of Nightstone died face-down in the dirt of the village he had stolen. And that ought to have been the end of it. But oaths in the Realms are heavier than iron. And Aganor, some days before, had sworn hers to Asmodeus himself.

## ACT TWO — The Fun Way

VANDAL: *(grave, unfolding)* The corpse had barely cooled when the ground split. Four imps of the Hells clawed up into the daylight. And behind them rose a tall figure in a silver breastplate, horned and courteous, who introduced himself — and I promise you, friend, I am not inventing this — as Destiny.

VANDAL: *(measured)* A cambion. Half devil, half something that was once mortal. And he had come on business. For Aganor's oath to Asmodeus had carried one condition — that she never raise her hand against Corvin. She had put a dagger in him. The contract, as devils reckon such things, was satisfied. Her soul was forfeit.

VANDAL: *(dry)* The crew asked what awaited her below. Destiny was happy to say. A few hundred years of torment. Then remaking as a slug-creature in the pits. Then — and here his voice went almost fond — she could work her way back up the ranks.

VANDAL: *(quoted, cold)* "Just as I did."

VANDAL: *(quickening)* They tried to bargain. To buy a delay, a loophole, anything. Devils love a deal, but only the ones they draft. And when Aganor's nerve broke and she lunged at the fiend herself — well. Then there was nothing left but the running of it. Toz threw a Fog Cloud over the cambion's head and stole his eyes. Grease went slick across the cobbles. Spike Growth tore up out of the earth between them and him. Fiz threw Cure Wounds after his friends as they backed toward the gate, and Destiny, screaming with frustration, lashed out blind — twin rays of hellfire through the fog, and one of them found Hal and burned him to the bone of his shoulder.

VANDAL: *(steadier)* But here is the thing about devils, friend. They want what the contract names, and nothing else moves them. Destiny had no appetite for four strangers. The fog held. The gate mechanisms were thrown. The crew slipped out into the dark and went to ground, breathing hard, listening for hooves that never came.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* When they crept back at last, the cambion was gone. And Aganor stood alive in the square — shaking, apologising, and telling the whole truth of her oath for the first time. Her soul is spoken for still. Mark that, friend. Devils are patient creditors.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT THREE — A Town Repaid

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Nightstone, by default, was free. And the village knew what it owed. Molak the innkeeper — retired adventurer, keeper of every secret worth keeping in that town — and the grateful villagers threw open their storerooms.

VANDAL: *(bright, telling)* Four draft horses and a wagon to pull behind them. A hundred and fifty gold pieces, eighty-five silver, and a good round sack of copper. Three gems worth fifty gold apiece. A potion of healing. A scroll of Continual Flame. Mason's tools. A cask of ale, food for the road — and one fine bottle of Dragon Breath whiskey, which I ask you to remember, friend, for it will surely matter someday.

VANDAL: *(conspiratorial)* Then Xolkin, the bandit captain who had lodged so long at Molak's inn, rolled back his sleeve — and showed them the winged snake inked on his arm. Zhentarim, friend. The Black Network. He muttered darkly of a woman named Kella, who had stolen something of his, and he pressed a magical item into the crew's hands as thanks — its nature unnamed, its powers unknown, and with it, unspoken, the shape of a debt.

VANDAL: *(quieter, reflective)* Fiz walked out to the great pit at the village heart, where the Nightstone itself once stood before the cloud giants tore it from the earth, and drew what small divinations he could from the empty ground. And Hal looked hard at the blade he had taken from the crypt, and learned at last what it was. Enchanted steel, true enough — a finer sword than any he had carried. But cursed. It seeps mistrust into the hand that holds it. And every elf who meets its bearer feels a coldness they cannot name.

VANDAL: *(low)* He kept it anyway. Paladins, friend, are stubborn in exactly that way.

## ACT FOUR — A Giant in Purple Robes

VANDAL: *(wondering, drawing close)* And then came the part of this tale I made them tell me twice. The four sat over a map, arguing the road ahead — Triboar, where a contact was said to know the true name of the genie in their salvaged bottle. Bryn Shandar, far up in the ice. Waterdeep, the City of Splendours. And as they argued, the sky above Nightstone — opened.

VANDAL: *(theatrical, storyteller)* Down out of the clouds came a tower. A flying tower, friend, stone and spire and all. And at its rail stood a cloud giant in robes of deep purple, with hair — I say this plainly, believe me or don't — hair made of living cloud. He named himself Zephyros. And he looked down at four souls not one of whom reached his knee, and said —

VANDAL: *(quoted, grave)* "You are the ones who will restore the Ordning. Or you will die. One of those things will happen. Perhaps both."

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* The Ordning. Mark the word, for it is the spine of everything that follows. It is the sacred ranking of all giantkind — storm above cloud, cloud above fire, fire above frost, down through stone and hill — set in place by their gods since the first days of the world. And it has been shattered. King Hekaton, lord of the storm giants, greatest of them all, has vanished into the sea near a place called Maelstrom. His daughters are young, and scrambling, and failing. And with the king gone, every giant lord in the North is clawing for the top of the heap.

VANDAL: *(darker)* It was Hekaton's own decree — *do not interfere with the small folk* — that kept the giants peaceful these many hundred years. That decree died with his voice. The hill giants gorge themselves for their queen. And the fire giants, Zephyros said, are unearthing the pieces of the Vonindod — a construct of iron, giant-shaped and vast, from the old wars against the dragons. Should they raise it, the Ordning will belong to fire, and the North will burn.

VANDAL: *(softer)* To reach Hekaton's daughters at Maelstrom, deep beneath the waves, one needs a conch of a giant lord — a magical shell that carries its bearer down where no ship can go. The crew has no such shell. Not yet.

VANDAL: *(amused, warm)* And Zephyros himself? He confessed, quite gently, that long years of consorting with beings of the outer planes through high magic had — his words — *affected his brain*. "But it has been worth it," he said. "Because here I am." And he opened the door of his flying tower, and offered them passage.

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so, friend, the prologue of this tale closes. Corvin is dust. Nightstone breathes free. Aganor lives, but her soul sits in a ledger in the Nine Hells, and a cambion named Destiny keeps patient books. Xolkin's grudge simmers on. Hal carries a blade that whispers mistrust into his palm. And above a scarred little village, a purple-robed giant holds open the door of a flying tower — waiting on four small heroes who have just been told they will restore the order of all giantkind, or die trying. Perhaps both.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The Storm King's thunder is only beginning.
