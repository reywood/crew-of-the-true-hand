# Tales of the True Hand — 2025-12-07
## The Name in the Bottle

[MUSIC: low ember bed; a slow drip of water on stone]

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Listen, friend. Deep beneath the hills of Ardeep Forest, past the bat-cave and the tight ways, there is a chamber the goblins would not enter. A stalactite hangs there from the roof of the world. And down the length of that stone — slowly, patiently, as if it had all the years it could ever want — something black was bleeding.

VANDAL: *(quiet)* A druid of the Emerald Enclave saw that chamber once, in a vision. He named it a great evil. And he would not set foot inside.

VANDAL: *(dropping)* That night, four heroes stood at the mouth of its passage — and watched the vision made real.

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — a bargain struck with a goblin king in a horned black hat, a monster the crew was wise enough to run from, and an old brass bottle that holds a wish, waiting on a name.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

## ACT ONE — The goblin in the horned hat

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture it. Past the bat-cave, deeper than the crew had yet dared, into a chamber that stank of dog and damp. And there he sat. A great goblin in a horned black hat, a javelin in one hand and a captive woman of Nightstone held under the other. Around him, lesser goblins with their ears flat. And at his boots, a pack of rats grown to the size of hounds. Giant rats, friend — he called them his babies. He meant it.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Three gold pieces a head, he said, for the villagers of Nightstone. And the crew's purses, laid end to end, would not have bought back a single child. So the talk turned, as it must, to trade. There was a trouble down one of his tunnels, said the goblin. A little slime. His lads were too spooked to face it. Deal with that, and every soul walks free.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Now, Hal Stormguard — paladin of the Oath of Vengeance, and a man who knows what a hostage is worth to the one holding her — Hal pressed hard: the women, the children, and the wounded go out first, or there is no deal at all. And beneath the haggling, quiet as a stream under ice, Woz laid a Guidance on the words — for a cleric of Eldath knows how to gentle a hard room.

VANDAL: *(gently)* The goblin gave an inch. The children could go. A tiefling woman was chosen from among the captives to lead them out — a woman who, I am told, knew every one of those little ones by name. The rest of the villagers stayed behind, collateral under a horned black hat.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT TWO — Snigbat and the stalactite

VANDAL: *(sly, amused)* Their guide, then, down to the trouble. A small, twitchy creature by the name of Snigbat. And Snigbat, friend, had scarcely walked ten paces before he began — in a whisper — to sell out his own master. Boz Hark, he called him. Boz Hark means to betray you. Boz Hark never meant to let a single villager walk. And — by the by — when this is all over, might Snigbat perhaps come with you?

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* He led them through a passage so tight the four had to squeeze along it single file, and out into a broad, cold chamber. And there it hung. Fiz knew it at once — the stalactite from the druid Windraven's vision, the great evil the Emerald Enclave would not go near. And down its side, a black ooze bled, slow as candle-wax.

VANDAL: *(drawing close, taut)* Fiz did what a Halruaan tinker does. He sent his Eldritch Cannon ahead of him — the Force Ballista, his little clockwork gunner — and it spat a bolt of force that tore a chunk out of the thing. It flinched. It seemed, for one hopeful heartbeat, to be hurt. And then it slid down off the stone. And a pseudopod slick with acid whipped out of it and smashed the ballista to pieces in a single blow.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Bottled in that tight passage, with the acid coming on and nowhere to swing a halberd — the crew called the retreat. Fiz spoke an Expeditious Retreat over his own boots, and the four went back out the way they had come. Single file. Backs to the stone.

VANDAL: *(dropping, personal)* And here, friend, is a thing worth marking. In the great tales, heroes stand. But wise heroes know the ground they stand on. Not every monster is meant to be fought the day you meet it. The ooze did not follow them out. It had no need to. It had all the years it could ever want.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT THREE — Betrayal at the cave mouth

VANDAL: *(cold, measured)* At the mouth of the tunnel, Boz Hark was waiting. And there would be no honouring the bargain now — from either side. The slime still hung where it had always hung. And Snigbat had told them true: the goblin king had never once meant to let his villagers walk.

VANDAL: *(plain)* So it came to steel.

VANDAL: *(rising)* Woz raised his holy symbol and called down his Channel Divinity — Charm Animals and Plants — and two of the great rats, the boss's own beloved babies, turned gentle as lambs and stepped out of the fight. Half the goblin's guard, gone in a breath. Hal waded in with the long reach of his halberd. Toz — small Toz Greenbottle, with the storm always a half-step behind his shoulder — loosed a Thunderwave up at the ledge where the boss stood, and the air itself cracked like a ship's mast going over. And Fiz worked from the back, Fire Bolt after Fire Bolt streaking down the cavern.

VANDAL: *(steadier)* Goblins fell. The horned hat rolled in the dirt. But no fight in a cave full of hostages ends clean, friend — one of the Nightstone guards fell in that melee, and two of the villagers with him, before the last of the goblins broke and scattered down the far tunnels into the dark.

VANDAL: *(warm, amused)* And Snigbat — Snigbat, who had turned on his old master the moment the crew held the line — was still standing there at the end of it. Asked if he might tag along. As a scout, you understand. Asked, hopefully, after a spiked club. There was some ribbing — "Stingbat," somebody tried, and somebody else liked it far too much. The name did not stick. The goblin did. The Crew of the True Hand had themselves a scout.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT FOUR — The name at the bottom of the bottle

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* With the cave quiet, Woz tried one last measure against the thing in the stalactite chamber. He sent the two charmed rats in to test it. They tore chunks from the ooze, brave little wretches — and its acid ate them alive for their trouble. Tooth and claw, plainly, are no answer to it. Mark that, friend. The ooze still hangs. The vision is not yet closed.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Then they rolled the boulder off Boz Hark's stash. A modest haul, by the measure of the tales. Twelve gold pieces, with loose silver and copper beneath. A matched silver salt-and-pepper set, of all things. Woodcarving tools in a leather case still dark with old blood — and no one asked whose. A spell scroll of Zone of Truth — mark that one too; truth is a heavy stone to carry into a room. And a wooden holy symbol of Silvanus, inlaid with gold. Woz took that one for himself — and why not? Eldath keeps the still waters and Silvanus keeps the wild wood; the two old powers have shared a border since before there were names for either.

VANDAL: *(softer, drawing close)* And then, in a quiet aside, they brought out the ornate bottle — the one salvaged from the wreck of the *True Hand* itself — and showed it to old Molak. Molak, the innkeeper of a village that is no village now. His adventuring days are behind him, and he confessed, with the smallest of smiles, that he had once meant to take the bottle up himself. To have the wish. Instead, he pressed it back into their hands. And he gave up, at last, the name he had been holding all this while.

VANDAL: *(quoted, hushed)* Derothra Shendrel. Of Triboar. North up the long road, past Waterdeep.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Speak the genie's true name, said Molak, and the bottle grants a wish. Derothra Shendrel knows the word. And the etching on the bottle, friend — cut in Primordial, the tongue of the elemental powers — reads thus:

VANDAL: *(quoted, low)* "To speak my name is to unmake chains."

VANDAL: *(quiet)* Whose chains, it does not say.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so the crew settled into the cleared cave, and slept a proper sleep at last, and woke the stronger for it — crossed into a new season of their skill, as we say in the trade. Ahead of them: the road north, through Waterdeep, on to Triboar and the woman who knows the word. Behind them: a stalactite still bleeding in the dark, a vision unanswered. Molak stays with his villagers, weighing whether to try Nightstone again while the Zhentarim squat in its ruins. And Snigbat sharpens his hopes on a spiked club he has not yet been given.

VANDAL: *(darker)* And one thing more. Word from Molak, passed along like a cloud crossing the sun — hill giants have been raiding the grain-silos down in the Golden Fields. There is an order to giantkind, friend, older than any kingdom of men, and in tales to come I will tell you how that order broke. For now, mark only this: when the ordering of the world goes, it is the hungriest who rise first.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The road waits for you at dawn.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
