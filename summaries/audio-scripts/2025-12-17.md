# Tales of the True Hand — 2025-12-17
## Black Banners over Nightstone

---

## [COLD OPEN — 25s]

[MUSIC: low ember bed; wind moving through pine]

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Listen, friend. There is a village in the High Forest that once flew the colours of a lady named Nandor. And when four heroes returned to its walls — the same walls they had helped to save — those colours were gone. In their place, black banners. Black as a raven's wing. Black as a crypt door left ajar. And beneath them, a stranger on the seat that a dead woman used to keep warm.

[STING: sharp low chord, held — 3s]

---

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met. Draw close. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — Nightstone under new colours, a tiefling's oath to the Prince of the Nine Hells, and a sword that did not wish to be lifted.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

---

## [ACT ONE — The Golden-Antlered Deer]

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* The morning found them where I left them last — camped near a goblin cave, foraging in the wood, the four of them stretching the sleep out of their bones. And in the trees, between one breath and the next, a deer. Only a deer, friend. Save that its antlers were of gold.

VANDAL: *(measured, wondering)* They did not chase it. They did not put an arrow to it. They marked it, and let it go. A small thing. A quiet omen. In these tales, friend, the quiet omens are the ones you remember.

VANDAL: *(shifting)* And when they came back down out of the wood, provisions in hand, and lifted their eyes to Nightstone — there flew the black banners. And the world, as it does, had moved without them.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT TWO — Corvin's Claim]

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* At the gate, a face they knew. Merrick, the guard. And Merrick had news, delivered plain as a man delivers bad weather. Lady Nandor was dead — killed in the cloud giant raid, the same raid these four had answered. And a man had come. Corvin. He carried royal blood, Merrick said. The claim was, by the letter of the law, honest.

VANDAL: *(dry)* Daphne, the lady-in-waiting — she confirmed it, with a mouth pulled tight as a purse-string. Technically within his rights, she said. Cold comfort, the way she said it.

VANDAL: *(unfolding)* The four were granted an audience. And Corvin — this new lord of Nightstone — was not much interested, it seemed, in the grievances of one small village. His eye was on a wider horizon. Frost giants harrying Bryn Shandar, far in the north. Fire giants digging strange, deep holes at Triboar, at the Golden Fields. The giant troubles, friend, were not one village's sorrow. They were the North's.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT THREE — Oaths in the Square]

VANDAL: *(gathering)* Corvin called for oaths. Every soul in Nightstone who meant to stay was to swear him loyalty in the square. Toz and Fiz went down to witness — halfling and gnome, watching the neighbours line up under the black banners to give away pieces of themselves.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Most swore plainly enough. A crop, a hearth, a roof, a lord. But one drew every eye — Aganor, a tiefling woman, who swore her oath not to Corvin, not to any earthly seat, but to Asmodeus. To the Prince of the Nine Hells himself.

VANDAL: *(dropping)* And a family of three — the Delfryndels — would not swear at all. They walked out past the gate. They chose the wild over words they would not say. Mark that too, friend. In these times, an unbought conscience is a rare coin.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Aganor had her own quarrel. Corvin's people had taken her house, and with it her spellcasting focus — the very shape through which her magic flows. Help me get it back, she told the two, and I will owe you. So Toz and Fiz sought out Xolkin — keeper of that troublesome flying snake you may remember — who had no love left for the new lord. And with his help, they slipped into the occupied house, and drew the focus out, and pressed it back into Aganor's hand.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* A debt owed, now. Uncollected. But owed.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## [ACT FOUR — The Crypt Beneath the Wall]

VANDAL: *(dropping into serious)* And while Toz and Fiz worked the village above, Hal and Woz — the paladin and the cleric of Eldath — went underneath it.

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* There was a passage. A secret way, opening from outside the walls into Nightstone's crypt. A door beneath the door, friend, which is the sort of door heroes are apt to find. It bypassed Corvin's guards entirely. They took it.

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Down among the stone dead they went, torch in hand, until they came to a sarcophagus. And Hal — Hal of Stormguard, oathsworn of vengeance — set his hands to the lid, and pried it.

VANDAL: *(quieter)* Within lay a sword. Finely made. More than steel. He took it up.

VANDAL: *(cold, quickening)* And the sword's old owner objected. Out of the stone rose a ghost. And the paladin and the cleric of the still waters fought that dead thing down between them, blade and staff, until the crypt was quiet again.

VANDAL: *(grave)* The sword was theirs. But when Hal closed his fist on the hilt, he felt what the blade brought with it. A chill. A whisper under the skin. He is a paladin, friend. He knows the shape of a curse when he holds one.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## [ACT FIVE — The Roads Out]

VANDAL: *(reflective)* And so, by evening, the four sat with what they had gathered. A tiefling's promise, half-paid. A sword that would ask a price for every swing. A village under a claimant whose right no one could quite deny, and whose rule no one quite loved. And two long roads pointing outward from Nightstone's gate.

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* North — to Bryn Shandar, where the frost giants press. West and south — to Triboar, to the Golden Fields, where the fire giants dig at the earth as if hunting for something buried there.

VANDAL: *(softer)* Which road, friend? The one where men are freezing, or the one where the earth is being opened? Four heroes must choose. And a golden-antlered deer, somewhere in the wood, watches to see which way they turn.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

---

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* A cursed sword in the hand of a vengeful man. A debt owed by a woman sworn to Asmodeus. A family walking out into the wild rather than kneel. A lord on a stolen chair, and a village that has not decided yet whether to keep him. This, friend, is what a day in the North looks like now.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. Two roads wait at the gate, and only one dawn to choose.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.

[MUSIC: outro theme, full swell, fade out — 6s]
