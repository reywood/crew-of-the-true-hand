# Tales of the True Hand — 2025-11-12
## The Cave Beneath the Green Hill

---

## [COLD OPEN — 25s]

[MUSIC: low ember bed; a lonely wind through pine boughs]

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Listen. In the hour before dawn, on a broken village called Nightstone, four heroes woke to the tapping of a small green thing at a shuttered window. A flying snake, friend — the eyes and ears of the bandit sleeping one floor above them. It looked. It knew their faces. And it slipped away into the dark.

VANDAL: *(cold, hushed)* By sunrise, the orcs were at the gate.

[STING: sharp low chord, held — 3s]

---

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — an account I have since gathered — the crew follow the trail of the giants into Ardeep Forest, and meet a druid at the mouth of a green hill.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

---

## [ACT ONE — Dawn at the ruined inn]

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture the ruined inn at Nightstone. Its townsfolk long fled into the woods. Its windows shuttered. Its watches turning quietly, hour to hour, through the dark. And upstairs — mark this — the bandit Xolkin, dreaming his own dreams, while his little green familiar came sniffing at the crew's window.

VANDAL: *(amused)* A sharp word, a spooked gesture — off it went. But the four knew the shape of it now. The man upstairs was more than a common thug.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Then came the orcs. Through the broken gate at dawn, a whole band of them — and the crew met them in the yard and cut them down before they reached the door. And the bandits above? *(dry)* The bandits, friend, kept very quiet indeed.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT TWO — The soldiers on the bridge]

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* Out into the morning, then, the djinn-bottle still tucked away for Molak, still undelivered. And there, on the keep's bridge — four figures, wrestling a wooden door across a fifteen-foot gap. Nightstone's own soldiers, penned inside their own keep, trying to build themselves a way out.

VANDAL: *(warm)* The moment they clocked the party, they scuttled back through the door like mice. But Fiz — small Fiz of Halruaa — coaxed one green recruit into speaking across the moat. And out came the tale at last.

VANDAL: *(grave)* Cloud giants. Down out of the sky. Boulders, dropped like a child drops stones into a pond. And when the dust cleared, the great black nightstone — the very stone that gave the place its name — was gone. Lifted away. Stolen.

VANDAL: *(dropping, softer)* And Lady Nandor, keeper of that keep — Lady Nandor lay dead in the rubble. Crushed by one of the falling stones. Her lady-in-waiting, taken with the other captives. Nightstone sacked three times over — giants, and goblins, and orcs, and then Xolkin's bandits sliding in on top. The guards had no stomach left to hold it.

VANDAL: *(sly, telling)* And the talk turned, as talk will, to that bounty out of Darkhope — the girl Kella, whose father is a great name in Darkar, and whose grandfather bears the name Teldor. The crew tucked those names away, friend. A thread for another day.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT THREE — The elf at the cave mouth]

VANDAL: *(warm, wondering)* Now — the guards were willing, when it came to it. Willing to ride out and follow the tracks. A mile into Ardeep Forest they went, past faint smears of blood on the trail, until the footprints ended at the mouth of a large cave beneath a green hill.

VANDAL: *(hushed)* And kneeling on a mat before that cave — an elf. In meditation. Stones drifting in slow orbit about his shoulders, as if the very ground had forgotten which way was down. Mid-ritual. Trying to divine what lay inside.

VANDAL: *(measured)* The crew did not break the spell. They joined it. Woz — Woz of the Eldath cleric's steady focus — lent his own quiet to the elf's, and together they saw.

VANDAL: *(unfolding, drawing close)* A pull-away view of Toril. A plunge down through tunnels, and tunnels beneath tunnels. And at the end of the plunge — a stalactite. A great stone tooth, thrumming with an evil presence.

VANDAL: *(warm)* The elf's name was Daniel Windraven, of the Emerald Enclave — a druidic order watching the sudden movements of giants across Faerûn. Cloud giants, he told them, stealing ancient artifacts from many places. Goblins falling in line, as goblins do, beneath bigger and meaner masters.

VANDAL: *(reflective)* He would not enter the cave himself. The danger was beyond him. But as a parting gift he pressed a living bracelet of vine onto Fiz's wrist — a Watch-token of the Enclave. It would wither, he said, on the day Fiz turned enemy. Otherwise it would fade of its own accord in an hour. And somewhere on the wind, he mentioned, a white owl was watching.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## [ACT FOUR — Drawing Thock out of the mud]

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Fiz and Woz crept ahead. Past the entrance the cave opened into a broad chamber: a mud pit at the centre, and in the mud pit an ogre wallowing, and along the stone shelves of the walls, goblins perched like carrion crows. Beyond a central rock formation — darkness, swallowing all the rest.

VANDAL: *(sly, telling)* The pair pulled back. They dropped the four soldiers into the treeline as hidden bait. And they shouted.

VANDAL: *(theatrical)* Out came Thock. Alone. Club dragging. Mud sliding off him in ropes. And the crew and their guards took him apart in the clearing before he could raise the club twice.

VANDAL: *(dropping)* Then came his mate, calling his name into the trees. A ballista bolt found her. A firebolt found her. She went down before she could close.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## [ACT FIVE — The pit of bats]

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* With the ogres dead, the goblins scattered deeper. The crew swept the front chambers by torchlight — mud, trinkets, side passages — and cornered a knot of non-combatants. Among them an elderly goblin woman, grey-haired and unhurt, only frightened.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Intimidated, she gave up what she knew. The villagers, she said, were in the Bat Cave — off in the far passage. And mind the bats, she warned. Mind the many bats.

VANDAL: *(drawing close)* And there the crew found them. The people of Nightstone, ringed around a deep pit that dropped into a lower chamber, the chirping of bats rising up out of the dark like a small dry rain. The villagers came out. One of them recognised the guards, and asked after Lady Nandor.

VANDAL: *(grave, softer)* The news, friend, broke poorly.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

---

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, closing)* And so a chapter closes at the mouth of a green hill in Ardeep. The villagers walked out. The ogres lay dead in the clearing. But the goblin boss remained in his deeper dark, and the stalactite — the great stone tooth Windraven's vision pointed to — still thrummed with something evil at the heart of the world.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Xolkin sleeps still in the Nightstone inn, and his flying snake has seen their faces. Molak's djinn-bottle is still undelivered. The Kella bounty waits, patient, at the edge of the map. And a druid of the Emerald Enclave now walks the woods with the crew's names in his mouth.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.

[MUSIC: outro theme, full swell, fade out — 6s]
