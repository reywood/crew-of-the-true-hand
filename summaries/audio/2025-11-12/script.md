# Tales of the True Hand — 2025-11-12
## The Stone the Giants Stole

A flying snake, friend. It watched four heroes sleeping. It learned their faces. VANDAL: *(cold, hushed)* By sunrise, the orcs were at the gate.

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

Tonight — an account I have gathered from those who lived it — the crew learn at last who broke Nightstone, and follow the trail into Ardeep Forest, to a cave beneath a green hill.

## ACT ONE — Dawn at the ruined inn

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture the ruined inn at Nightstone. The townsfolk long fled into the woods. The windows shuttered, the doors barred, the watches turning quietly, hour to hour, through the dark. Upstairs, the bandit Xolkin and his hired blades, sleeping the sleep of men who believe the town is theirs. And at the window — that small green visitor.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Fiz woke to it first. And being Fiz — small Fiz of Halruaa, tinker and artificer — he did not cry out. He raised a hand and began the words and gestures of a casting, and the sound alone sent the snake pouring off the sill and away into the night.

VANDAL: *(conspiratorial)* Now mark this, friend. On the Sword Coast, a winged serpent at your window is seldom only a serpent. There is a company in the Realms that flies that very signature — I'll not name it tonight; it names itself soon enough. But the man upstairs, the crew now knew, was more than a common thug.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Then came the dawn, and with the dawn came orcs. A whole band of them, pouring through Nightstone's broken gate with slaughter in mind. The crew met them in the yard and cut them down before a single one reached the inn door. And the bandits above, with all their swords and swagger? *(dry)* The bandits, friend, kept very quiet indeed.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT TWO — The soldiers on the bridge

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* Out into the morning, then — the ornate genie-bottle still undelivered in the pack, the promise to the innkeeper Molak still unkept. And there, on the bridge of Nightstone's keep — four figures, wrestling a wooden door across a fifteen-foot gap of empty air. Nightstone's own soldiers. Penned inside their own keep, building themselves a way out of it.

VANDAL: *(amused)* The moment they clocked the party, they scuttled back inside like mice under a lantern. But Fiz has a gift for talking to frightened things. He coaxed one green recruit — helmet down over his eyes, voice cracking — into speaking across the moat. And out, at last, came the tale of what had broken Nightstone.

VANDAL: *(grave)* Cloud giants. Down out of the sky. Boulders, dropped from the clouds the way a cruel child drops stones into a pond. And when the dust settled and the screaming stopped, the great black nightstone — the sacred stone in the town square, the very stone that gave the village its name — was gone. Lifted up. Carried off into the sky.

VANDAL: *(dropping, softer)* And Lady Nandor, the keeper of that keep — Lady Nandor lay dead in the rubble, crushed beneath one of the falling stones. Her lady-in-waiting carried off with the other captives. Count it, friend: giants, then goblins, then orcs, then Xolkin's bandits on top. Nightstone sacked three times over — and the surviving guards had no stomach left for holding it.

VANDAL: *(wondering, aside)* Why would cloud giants — the loftiest of all giantkind — stoop to smashing a village for one black stone? There is a word that answers it. The word is Ordning. But that word belongs to a later tale, and I'll not rob it tonight.

VANDAL: *(sly, telling)* And the talk at the moat's edge turned, as talk will, to bounty. The girl Kella, hunted out of Darkhope — her father a famous name in Darkar, her grandfather called Teldor. The crew tucked those names away like coins in a boot. A thread for another day.

[STING: chime — 1s]

## ACT THREE — The elf at the cave mouth

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* The guards, to their credit, found their nerve when it mattered. They rode out with the crew, following footprints past faint smears of blood on the trail, a mile deep into Ardeep Forest, until the tracks ended at the mouth of a large cave beneath a green hill.

VANDAL: *(hushed)* And kneeling on a mat before that cave — an elf. In meditation. Stones drifting in slow orbit about his shoulders, as though the ground beneath him had quietly forgotten which way was down. He was mid-ritual, and had been for some while — trying, and failing, to divine what lay inside the hill.

VANDAL: *(measured)* The crew did not break the spell. Mark that, friend — how many would have? Instead, they joined it. Woz — Woz of the deep woods, cleric of Eldath, the goddess of still waters — lent his quiet to the elf's quiet, and the ritual that would not close, closed.

VANDAL: *(unfolding, drawing close)* And together, they saw. All of Toril, pulling away beneath them, as if they had been snatched up into the sky. Then a plunge — down through tunnels, and tunnels beneath tunnels, faster than thought. And at the bottom of the plunge: a stalactite. A great stone tooth hanging in the dark, thrumming with an evil presence — and off it rolled a wave of fear and anguish, and the screams of every soul that thing had ever swallowed.

VANDAL: *(warm)* The elf's name was Daniel Windraven, of the Emerald Enclave — that far-flung fellowship of druids and rangers that keeps the balance of the wild across Faerûn. His circle had been tracking the sudden movements of giants all over the North, and everywhere the same account came back: giants, stealing ancient artifacts, and no one could say why. As for the goblins in the cave — goblins, he said, fall in line beneath bigger and meaner masters. They always have.

VANDAL: *(reflective)* He would not enter the cave himself; the danger inside was beyond him. But as a parting gift he pressed a living circlet of vine onto Fiz's wrist — a Watch-token of the Enclave. It would wither, he said, on the day Fiz turned enemy to the wild. Otherwise it would simply fade in an hour or so, as growing things do.

VANDAL: *(amused, warm)* And Fiz — never in his life out-gifted — worked his Magical Tinkering on the spot, and pressed into the druid's palm a tiny clockwork owl, glowing ember-orange.

VANDAL: *(quoted, bright)* "This marks you as a friend of mine. It'll fade in an hour or so."

VANDAL: *(gently)* Two tokens, friend, and neither built to outlast the afternoon. I would wager the friendship outlasts us both.

VANDAL: *(low)* One more thing the druid mentioned, almost in passing — a white owl riding the wind above Ardeep. Not a wild bird. A familiar, conjured of spellcraft, watching on its master's behalf.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT FOUR — Drawing Thock from the mud

VANDAL: *(quickening)* Fiz and Woz crept ahead to scout, and what they saw past the cave mouth they carried back out on held breath. A broad chamber. A mud pit at its centre, and in the mud pit an ogre, wallowing. Goblins perched along the stone shelves of the walls like carrion crows. And beyond a central pillar of rock — darkness, swallowing all the rest.

VANDAL: *(sly, telling)* Now, four heroes and four borrowed soldiers do not storm a cave like that. They set a table for it. The soldiers went down into the treeline as hidden bait, crossbows ready. Fiz planted his Eldritch Cannon in the grass — the squat little Force Ballista, humming to itself. And then, friend, they shouted.

VANDAL: *(theatrical)* Out came Thock. Alone. Club dragging, mud sliding off him in ropes, blinking into the daylight. And the crew and their hidden guards took the ogre apart in the clearing before he could raise that club a second time.

VANDAL: *(dropping)* Then a second voice from the cave mouth — his mate, calling his name into the trees. She came on at a run. Woz's Sacred Flame fell on her like judgment. Thorns of Spike Growth erupted from the earth across her path. A Fire Bolt from Fiz caught her burning, and the Force Ballista's bolt struck her square and hurled her backward across the thorns. She went down before she ever came close enough to swing.

VANDAL: *(quiet)* Two ogres dead in a green clearing. In the great tales, friend, it is always dragons. In most true ones, it is mud, and ogres, and someone clever enough to make the monster come to them.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

## ACT FIVE — The pit of bats

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* With the ogres dead, the goblins scattered deeper, and the crew went in after them — sweeping the front chambers by torchlight, through mud and trinkets and side passages. In one corner they ran a knot of non-combatants to ground. Among them, an elderly goblin woman, long grey hair, frightened and unhurt.

VANDAL: *(warm)* She was persuaded — firmly, mind you, but no rougher than that — and she gave up what she knew. The villagers of Nightstone were being kept in the Bat Cave, off along the far passage. And mind the bats, she warned them. Mind the many bats.

VANDAL: *(drawing close)* And there they found them at last. The stolen folk of Nightstone, ringed around the lip of a deep pit that dropped away into a lower chamber, the chirping of bats rising out of that dark like a small dry rain. The crew brought them out — every one of them — up out of the hill and into the light.

VANDAL: *(softer)* One of the villagers knew the soldiers by name. And she asked, as they walked, after Lady Nandor.

VANDAL: *(grave, softer)* The news, friend, broke poorly. It always does.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, closing)* And so a chapter closes at the mouth of a green hill in Ardeep. The villagers walked free. The ogres lay dead in the clearing. But the goblins' boss still held the deeper dark, unmet — and far below it all, if the druid's vision told true, that great stone tooth went on thrumming with its old evil, patient as only stone can be.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Xolkin sleeps yet in the Nightstone inn, and his winged snake knows four faces now. Molak's genie-bottle rides undelivered in the pack. The Kella bounty waits at the edge of the map — Darkhope, Darkar, and a grandfather named Teldor. And somewhere in the wilds of Faerûn, a druid of the Emerald Enclave walks with a clockwork owl in his memory and the crew's names in his mouth.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The cave is not finished with you.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
