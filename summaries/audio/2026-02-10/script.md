## The Blue Dragon at Goldenfields

There came a dream, in the last quiet hour before the storm, of a glassy sea with no horizon at all — and something vast beneath it, stirring for the first time. And out of that dark water a voice reached up to a small halfling captain, and told him his god of the sea no longer watched over him.

VANDAL: *(quoted, cold, hushed)* "Worship me, and my power will be yours. To drown is to be reborn."

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — an account I have since gathered, from the four themselves and from a purple-robed giant who lived to tell it — a blue dragon comes down out of a storm that never should have been, and the crew set their heels in the mud of Goldenfields to meet him.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

---

## [ACT ONE — A warning on the wind]

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture it. A cloud tower drifting the last miles toward the granary-fields, and inside, four heroes taking what they thought was a well-earned rest. Toz Greenbottle, halfling captain of the wrecked *True Hand*, closed his eyes on the wind. And in his sleep the glassy ocean rose again to meet him. But this time, friend — this time — a shape moved beneath the water. Vast. Malevolent. And it spoke.

VANDAL: *(quoted, cold, hushed)* "To drown is to be reborn."

VANDAL: *(dropping into serious)* Toz woke to find Zephyros himself already at his shoulder. The cloud giant had cast Contact Other Plane in the dark hours, and reached across the void to friends he keeps out there. And what came back to him was this. That the giant gods — the old ones, the true ones — wished to aid the crew. But not yet. Not before the four had proven themselves at a place called the Eye of the All-Father. The Eye of Annam. Where an oracle waits who can answer any question set before her.

VANDAL: *(measured)* And there was more. Great peril, Zephyros said. Bearing down. Already on the wind.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT TWO — Down to the ground]

VANDAL: *(taut, unfolding)* Out on the horizon, then. A storm. But not any storm. Bluish-mauve, laced with lightning that walked sideways through the cloud. An hour and a half out, and closing.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Toz, sensible captain that he is, put it plain. A tower a thousand feet in the air is a murder-box when murder comes flying. Set us down. And Zephyros, without argument, sank his spire to a rocky outcrop beside a small wooded grove, with the walled town of Goldenfields visible across the fields beyond.

VANDAL: *(amused, softer)* A farmer's wagon on the road took one look at the tower coming down out of the sky, and wheeled about, and fled. Fair play to him, friend. I would have done the same.

VANDAL: *(unfolding)* Fiz — small Fiz of Halruaa — sent a charmed griffin up on the wind to scout. And back it came with the news. Three gargoyles of stone. A little flying lizard, kobold-sized. And, riding at the head of them all, a great blue dragon, with a man upon his back. A half-dragon in a deep blue cloak.

VANDAL: *(dropping)* So the crew dug in. Zephyros's great desk overturned for cover. His levitating disc set as a wall. Hal on the upper floor with his halberd. Woz laying Bless and Barkskin over the four in turn. And the doorway held.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT THREE — The dragon and his rider]

VANDAL: *(grave, quickening)* The dragon landed with a thud that shook the outcrop. And he named his price at once — the giant's blood. There would be no parley. Fiz tried; the beast would not hear. And Zephyros, purple-robed and grim, admitted plainly to the four that this would be his first true dragon-fight.

VANDAL: *(urgent)* The half-dragon rider had his own designs. Ethereal wings unfurled at his back, and he broke off from the beast to fly for the tower's upper floors — some errand of his own, unspoken. But Fiz, small Fiz, took him with a Firebolt clean across the sky. His concentration broke. His wings failed. And down he came, sixty feet, into the wet grass.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Toz was upon him before he could rise. A dagger to the heart. And at the half-dragon's throat, a glass vial of blue liquid turned to ash in the same breath — as if some pact had been unwritten with his dying.

VANDAL: *(taut)* The dragon answered. A line of lightning as thick as a mast, straight at Zephyros. The giant shrugged it off. But out on the wing of the fight, the gargoyles took one of the griffins and tore it to bloody ribbons in the rain.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## [ACT FOUR — A bloody trade in the rain]

VANDAL: *(unfolding, quickening)* And so it ground on, friend. Zephyros called down an air elemental — a whirlwind at the dragon's flank. Woz's Guiding Bolts came, one and then another, painting the beast in radiant light. Toz laid Ray of Frost on the little kobold lizard. And Hal — Hal, oath-sworn Hal — leapt nine feet straight up into the storm and drove his blade into the dragon's eye.

VANDAL: *(dropping, taut)* The dragon's breath weapon recharged. And Hal went down. Straight down. A griffin dead beside him, and the paladin rolling death saves in the mud while gargoyles raked at Fiz across the outcrop.

VANDAL: *(urgent)* But the crew held. A javelin found the dragon's throat. A flurry of Zephyros's magic missiles found his heart. And a final ranged blow, from I know not whose hand, brought that great blue mountain of a beast crashing down into the wet earth, dead.

VANDAL: *(softer, warm)* Zephyros, with a giant's courtesy, Mass Suggested the last gargoyle politely out of his tower. Woz laid a healing word on Hal, and Hal opened his eyes to the rain.

VANDAL: *(measured)* They had lived, friend. Bloodied, and one griffin the poorer. But they had lived.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## [ACT FIVE — The spoils, and the questions]

VANDAL: *(warm, telling)* Now — the picking of a dragon, friend, is an old trade. The crew set to it in the rain.

VANDAL: *(unfolding)* From the half-dragon's body, Toz took two prizes. A Bloodwell Vial — a sorcerer's focus, that grants a keen edge to a spell attack and restores sorcery to its bearer on a short rest. And a Dagger of Venom besides. The kobold's satchel — small, and immaculate, and heavier by far than it should have been — proved a Bag of Holding. Inside it: eighty gold. A potion of lightning resistance. Two scrolls of Tongues. Blue-steel thieves' tools etched in Draconic numerals. A silk pouch of powdered sapphire, worth a hundred and fifty gold at a fair table. A non-magical ring, cut with the sigil of a blue dragon. And a sheaf of notes in Draconic — that no small hand aboard could yet read.

VANDAL: *(amused, softer)* Two liters of the dragon's own blood, they drew off into a giant-sized jar. Zephyros warned them, plain — a potent stimulant. Highly addictive. But a fine thing to sell to the right buyer. Fiz pocketed a single intact tooth as a keepsake. And the crew, drenched and mud-caked, felt the road turn under their feet — for they had crossed into a new season of their skill.

VANDAL: *(dropping, reflective)* But mark the questions, friend. The half-dragon had aims of his own. His vial of ash. His unread notes. A ring bearing a sigil no one has yet claimed. And beneath the sea, in Toz's sleep, a shape that offers what a drowning god cannot.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so a chapter closes, in the rain outside the walls of Goldenfields, beside the corpse of a dragon and the tower of a giant who has now, for the first time, seen one fall. Ahead of the four — the Eye of Annam, wherever it may lie, where an oracle waits with an answer to any question a small hand can bring. Ahead of them — Goldenfields itself, walled and watching. And behind Toz's eyes, still, a glassy sea, and a shape beneath it that has begun to speak.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The road waits for you at dawn — and today, the road runs through mud.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
