# Tales of the True Hand — 2025-12-07
## The Bargain in the Bat Cave

---

## [COLD OPEN — 25s]

[MUSIC: low ember bed; a slow drip of water on stone]

VANDAL: *(hushed, drawing close)* Listen. Deep beneath the hills near Nightstone, in a chamber the goblins were too frightened to enter, a stalactite hangs from the roof of the world. And down the length of that stone — slowly, patiently, as if it has all the years it could ever want — a black slime bleeds. A druid once saw it in a vision and called it a great evil. That night, four heroes stood at the mouth of its passage and saw the vision made real.

[STING: sharp low chord, held — 3s]

---

## [TITLE — 8s]

[MUSIC: signature theme, brief]

VANDAL: *(bright, storyteller)* Well met, friend. Draw close to the fire. I am Vandal Lovelace, and this is a Tale of the True Hand. Tonight — a bargain with a goblin king in a horned black hat, a monster the crew were wise enough to run from, and a name whispered over a bottle that holds a wish.

[MUSIC: settles under, becomes bed]

---

## [ACT ONE — The goblin in the horned hat]

VANDAL: *(warm, unfolding)* Picture it. Past the bat cave, past the tight ways, into a chamber that stank of dog and damp. There he sat — a great goblin in a horned black hat, javelin in one hand, a captive woman held under the other. About him, lesser goblins. And at his boot, a pack of rats grown to the size of hounds. He called them his babies. He meant it.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Three gold a head, he said, for the villagers of Nightstone. The crew's purses would not have bought a single child. So the talk turned, as it must, to trade. There was a trouble down one of his tunnels, he said. A little slime. His lads were too spooked to face it. Take care of it, and every soul walks free.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Hal — Hal Stormguard, paladin of Vengeance — pressed for the wounded and the children first. And Woz — Woz of the Greenbottles, cleric of Eldath — laid a whisper of guidance beneath the bargain. And so it was that the little ones went out ahead, led by a tiefling woman who knew every one of them by name. The rest stayed, collateral in the goblin's hand.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT TWO — Snigbat and the stalactite]

VANDAL: *(sly, amused)* Their guide, then. A small, twitchy thing by the name of Snigbat. He had scarcely walked ten paces with them before he began, in a whisper, to sell out his master. "Boz Hark," he called him. Meant to betray them, he said. Meant never to let a single villager walk. And by the by — could he perhaps come with them, when this was done?

VANDAL: *(measured, unfolding)* He led them through a passage so tight the four had to squeeze through single file — and out into a broad, cold chamber. And there it hung. Fiz knew it at once. The stalactite from the druid Windraven's vision. The great evil the Emerald Enclave would not enter.

VANDAL: *(drawing close, taut)* Down the stone, the black ooze slid. Fiz sent his force ballista in — his own small clockwork gunner — and a bolt of force tore a chunk out of the thing. It hurt. It seemed to hurt. And then it slid to the floor. And then it whipped out a pseudopod slick with acid and, in a single blow, punched the ballista to pieces. Sixteen points of damage, friend, on a machine barely bigger than a hound.

VANDAL: *(dropping, personal)* And here — here — is a thing worth marking. In the great tales, heroes stand. But wise heroes know the ground. Bottled up in that tight passage, with acid coming for them, the four called the retreat and shuffled out the way they had come. Single file. Backs to the wall. Not every monster is meant to be fought the day you meet it. The ooze, blessedly, did not follow. It had all the years it could ever want.

[STING: bridge — 2s]

---

## [ACT THREE — Betrayal at the mouth]

VANDAL: *(cold, measured)* At the tunnel's mouth, Boz Hark was waiting. There would be no bargain now — the slime was still up there, dripping. And Snigbat had told them true. He had never meant to let the villagers walk.

VANDAL: *(quickening)* So — initiative. Woz called on the wild and turned two of the great rats charmed, and they rounded on their master. The paladin waded in. Toz — Toz of the Greenbottles, storm in his palms — cracked the air. Fiz worked from the back with dart and spell. Goblins fell. A guard fell. And, to the crew's grief, two of the villagers fell with him before the last of the goblins broke and scattered into the dark.

VANDAL: *(warm, amused)* And Snigbat — Snigbat, who had turned coat when the crew held the line — was still there at the end. Asked if he might tag along. Asked for a spiked club. There was some ribbing, I am told. "Stingbat," someone called him. The name did not stick. But the goblin did. The Crew of the True Hand had picked up a scout.

[STING: chime — 1s]

---

## [ACT FOUR — The name at the bottom of the bottle]

VANDAL: *(measured, telling)* With the cave quiet, Woz sent the two charmed rats back into the stalactite chamber to test the thing. They tore chunks from it before the acid ate them alive. Melee, plainly, was no answer. Mark that for later, friend. The ooze still hangs. The vision is not yet closed.

VANDAL: *(warm)* They rolled the boulder from the boss's stash. A modest haul, by the measure of the tales — twelve gold, silver and copper below it, a matched set of silver salt and pepper. A set of woodcarving tools in a case still bloodied. A wooden holy symbol of Silvanus, inlaid with gold — Woz took that one, and set it beside his Eldath. And a spell scroll: Zone of Truth. Mark that one too. Truth is a heavy stone to carry into a room.

VANDAL: *(softer, drawing close)* And then, in a quiet aside, they showed old Molak the ornate bottle. Molak, the innkeeper of a village that is now no village. His adventuring days are behind him. He confessed — with the smallest of smiles — that he had meant to take the bottle up himself, once. Instead, he pressed it back into their hands. And he gave up, at last, the name he had been holding.

VANDAL: *(quoted, hushed)* Derothra Shendrel. Of Triboar. North up the road, past Waterdeep.

VANDAL: *(measured)* Speak the genie's true name, Molak said. And the bottle grants a wish. Derothra Shendrel knows the word.

[MUSIC: minor swell — 4s]

## [CLOSING — 30s]

VANDAL: *(reflective, drawing to a close)* And so the crew settled into the cleared cave, and slept a proper sleep, and woke — as heroes sometimes do — a little more than they had been. Level four, if you'll forgive a bard the bookkeeping. Ahead of them: the road north, through Waterdeep, on to Triboar. A wish, if they can find the word. A stalactite, still bleeding, that they will one day have to answer for. Molak with the villagers, deciding whether to try Nightstone again while the Zhentarim squat in it. And, high above the Golden Fields, hill giants at the silos.

VANDAL: *(warm)* Rest well tonight, heroes. The road waits for you at dawn.

VANDAL: *(warm, signature)* I am Vandal Lovelace. This has been a Tale of the True Hand.
