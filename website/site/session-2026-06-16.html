Session 2026-06-16

In brief: The crew arrives in Waterdeep, becomes minor celebrities, gathers an armful of leads, and stumbles into a mind-control attack at the docks.

At the gate

Riding into the City of Splendors, the crew was recognised at the gate — their faces had landed on the cover of the Waterdeep Wazoo. The guard let them in once they signed a copy of the paper for him in exchange for his own.

Just ahead of them a goliath named Cormeer and his wife were being turned away. The guard had mistaken Cormeer for a giant, and his protests that he was only on his way to work for a man named Brindle were not landing. Fiz vouched for him as a goliath, not a giant, and the guard let them through.

Chazlauth's drawing room

The crew met Chazlauth with Naxene's introduction in hand. Chaz turned out to be a flamboyant dragon enthusiast — he keeps a small silver dragon as a pet, which is illegal, and he is wholly untroubled by that fact. Pressed about Force Gray, he pointed the crew at the bard Vandal Lovelace, said to haunt the statue of Harshnag in the city. He mentioned that Laeral Silverhand rules Waterdeep, told them about the green dragon Old Gnawbone in Kryptgarden Forest as a potential giant-killing ally, and warned about the copper dragon Vexalanthus patrolling the High Road as an old Force Gray ally. Before they left, each of the crew received a potion of poison resistance.

Naxene offered them a guest house to stay in. Chaz recommended the Yawning Portal, said to be the most popular tavern in Waterdeep.

The bard at the statue

Vandal Lovelace was as promised: lounging at the foot of the Harshnag statue. He told them Harshnag was likely in Silverymoon, that the Harpers could get them there through a secret portal network, and that the Harper to find was Yackerty, a dwarf working out of the Trades Ward. Fiz noted he had an old Harper friend from Halruaa as a backup contact. The Harpers, Vandal said, are a poorly organized lot — identified by harp jewelry or tattoos.

Vandal also confirmed that Harshnag could lead them to the Oracle — and that the Oracle would help them find a giant conch. A dragon, he guessed, might be the way to get the conch from the giant. Bonus: Vandal knew Ol' Rourke. Said he was working the docks.

The crew slept at Naxene's guest house.

A mechanical statue and an alchemy jug

The next morning, walking through the streets, the crew watched a two-story walking mechanical statue — built by a man named Garley — fail and start to topple toward a crowd. Toz and Fiz caught it with Feather Fall. Fiz diagnosed the mechanical fault. Garley, grateful, gave him a magical alchemy jug pulled from the statue's belly.

The Deep Water Inn

They tracked Ol' Rourke to the Deep Water Inn down at the docks. Along the way Toz stopped at a Valkur temple and accepted a holy book from the priest.

Ol' Rourke was mid-tale, holding court with the story of the giant attack on the True Hand. He told the crew that Brindle might be able to set them up with a new ship.

Then, mid-story, a small group of tavern patrons went silent. Their eyes turned white. They stood and attacked. The crew spotted the instigator — who declared, before they put him down, "We're going to take back what's theirs." With his death the others snapped out of it. The phrase hung in the room.

What's next

  • Find Yackerty, the dwarf Harper in the Trades Ward.
  • Decide whether Old Gnawbone or Vexalanthus is the dragon-ally to approach first.
  • Take Brindle up on his help with a new ship.
  • Sit on the mind-control attack — someone, somewhere, has plans for what is "theirs."
