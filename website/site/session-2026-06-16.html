In brief: Making landfall in Waterdeep, the Crew found themselves front-page news, chased leads on Harshnag and the Oracle through a dragon-mad wizard and a bard by his statue, then broke a mind-controlled bar brawl at the docks.

Arrival and the Wazoo

The crew rolled into Waterdeep through the south gate to find their own faces staring back from the front page of the Waterdeep Wazoo under the headline "Giant Killers" — a sketch of the Golden Fields battle with all five of them, Naxene included, flinging spells and arrows. The gate guard, star-struck, traded a signed copy for one of the party's; Fiz sweetened it by zapping a magical message onto the guard's paper with an artificer trick (it fades in about three days), and the paper proved doubly useful for the city map printed inside. Toz brandished a giant-thumb trophy for good measure. In the next lane the Watch were turning away a very tall man — "you giant scum" — until Fiz's sharp eye (a natural-20 History check) marked him as a goliath, giant-kin but no giant, pleading a dock job waiting under a man named Brindle. Fiz vouched for him (Persuasion), and the couple was let through — the first time Brindle's name would land that day, though not the last.

Chazlauth's dragons

Naxene walked them to the tower of Chazlauth, a wizard so consumed by dragonlore that an illegally-kept silver wyrmling bolted out the door and had to be corralled with a gust of wind and a patch of grease before he could catch it. Over the recovered hatchling he answered their questions on Force Grey — Waterdeep's onetime adventuring company of Harshnag, Laeral Silverhand (now Open Lord), the bard Vandal Lovelace, and a copper dragon ally — and pressed on them a lead that might matter more than any bard: Old Gnawbone, an ancient green dragon in Kryptgarden Forest who hates giants enough to be worth courting, which he marked on their map. He also named Vexalanthus, a copper dragon patrolling the High Road, flagged their earlier blue-dragon fight as suspiciously unnatural for so young a wyrm, and laid out the shape of the road ahead: Harshnag to the Oracle, the Oracle to a giant's conch. Each of them left with a potion of poison resistance for the Gnawbone visit, and Naxene offered her family guest house for lodgings.

Vandal Lovelace at the statue

At Harshnag's statue — dragon-skull helm, two-bladed axe, "DEATH TO GIANTS" daubed across the base — a drunk bard sang the frost giant's praises on loop until Toz heckled him. That was Vandal Lovelace, himself a former Grey Hand, who sang them the rest: Harshnag was last heard heading for Silverymoon, reachable fast only through the Harpers and their secret teleportation circles. Fiz filled in the order's lore (a strong History check surfacing his own old Harper friend from Halruaa), and Toz talked Vandal into an introduction to Yackerty, a dwarf Harper in the Trades Ward. Vandal confirmed the copper dragon Vexalanthus as a trustworthy ally and pointed them to their old ship's navigator, Ol' Rourke, working the docks — then lost a friendly dagger-throwing wager to Fiz before the party stood him a pint.

Feather-falling a statue

Come morning — after Toz slept badly again, dreaming of black water, a voice, and a vast shape moving beneath — a two-story bronze walking statue ground its gears and toppled toward a crowd. Fiz's feather fall couldn't hold the mass alone, so Toz stacked a second feather fall on top and the machine drifted down softly. Fiz then out-tinkered its maker to find the fault; the builder, Garley Gond, rewarded him gnome-fashion with the leaking magical bladder from the statue's guts — which Fiz's eye read at once as an alchemy jug — and a bag of goodberries besides.

Valkur's Wave Hall and a dockside ambush

On the way to the docks Toz slipped into a timbered Wave Hall of Valkur, confessed his growing dread of water to the priest, swore himself to the god, and left with a holy book and daily penance to work through. At the Deepwater Inn they found Ol' Rourke mid-tale about the stone giants that sank the True Hand; he pointed them to Brindle Stormtide, a halfling ship-seller with a cheap vessel and "strings attached" — the same Brindle from the gate. Before they could talk business a waitress's eyes went milk-white and she rasped in the voice from Toz's nightmares, "I should have taken…" — and a dozen patrons and two of the Watch turned with her. Woz's keen read (Perception 25) marked them as ordinary people magically dominated. Fiz's detect magic exposed the puppeteer — one unremarkable man channeling the enchantment; Hal dropped him to a thread with two natural-20 javelins, and Toz swung his gust of wind around and finished him with a crossbow bolt. The instant the instigator fell the spell broke — the crowd came to, screaming and bewildered. Toz shaped water from a barrel to douse the barstool fire his wind had spread, and Woz knelt to heal the woman who'd started it. Whether the ringleader can be revived for questioning is a problem for next time.
