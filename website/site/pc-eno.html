Backstory

Born in the wilds, mostly raised by a mother hardened by solitude. His mother raised him with a simple creed: nature was not cruel, but it was never kind — it was something to be endured.

One particularly unkind season, a Greenbottle trade ship became lost and wandering inland. Eno’s mother found them and guided them to safety. In gratitude the Greenbottle's took young Eno into their fold. For several years he lived and worked among them. Once Eno came of age the wilds called him back.

After a time back in the wilderness he thought he knew, he stumbled upon a circle of druids who seemed to walk through the seasons without scars. Where he had endured, they simply belonged. Their peace humbled him, and through them he found Eldath, the quiet goddess of still waters. For the first time, survival became more than just endurance — it became balance.

Now, Eno finds himself once again on the sea, escorting a shipment to the frozen north.
